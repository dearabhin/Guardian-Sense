The Flaw in Modern Fall Detectors

While essential, fall detection systems are plagued by false positives. Even "smart" solutions with user confirmation fail when a user is unconscious or unable to respond, creating a critical gap in safety.

The "Cry-Wolf" Effect

Accidental drops and jolts trigger frequent false alarms, desensitizing caregivers and eroding trust in the system. When every alert is treated as a potential non-emergency, the response to a real one can be dangerously slow.

The Unresponsive User Problem

Systems that rely on a user to press a button to cancel an alert are fundamentally flawed. In the most severe falls, the user may be unable to respond, rendering the confirmation feature useless and delaying help when it's needed most.

Our Solution: A Two-Device AI Verification System

We built an intelligent, communicating ecosystem. A lightweight wearable detects a potential fall, and a stationary AI monitor visually confirms it before sending an alert.

Device A: The Wearable Pendant

A compact, non-intrusive device worn by the user. Its sole job is to detect a potential fall using a tilt sensor and instantly signal the AI Monitor.

Device B: The AI Monitor

A stationary unit with a camera that sits on a shelf. It houses our Edge Impulse ML model, trained to distinguish between real falls and normal postures.

Real-Time Communication Workflow

1

Potential Fall

The wearable's tilt sensor is triggered and it instantly sends a "CHECK_FALL" signal to the AI Monitor via ESP-NOW.

2

AI Verification

The AI Monitor wakes, captures an image, and runs its ML model to classify the scene as either a `fall` or `normal`.

3

Confirmed Alert

Only if the AI confirms a fall is a Telegram alert sent to the caregiver. False alarms are silently ignored.
